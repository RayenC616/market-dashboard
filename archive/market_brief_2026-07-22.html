Cross-Cutting Issues — Institutional Watch Items

U.S.-Iran conflict and Strait of Hormuz risk. The U.S. conducted an 11th consecutive night of airstrikes on Iran on July 22, expanding the bombing campaign to the northwest city of Tabriz for the first time and prompting President Trump to threaten strikes on Iran's "Pickaxe Mountain" nuclear site. Brent crude briefly topped $95/bbl intraday. Yemen's Houthis separately declared an embargo on vessels transiting the Bab el-Mandeb strait, raising the prospect of blockages at two of the world's most important oil chokepoints simultaneously. Institutional investors should monitor refining margins, tanker/freight rates, and headline energy-driven inflation pass-through over the coming weeks (sources: Northern Miner, Investopedia, WSJ/Yahoo Finance live coverage).

AI capex "show-me" phase begins with Alphabet and Tesla earnings. July 22 marked the first two Magnificent Seven earnings reports of the season. Alphabet beat on revenue and EPS but fell ~4.7% after hours on capex-growth concerns, while Tesla's results missed consensus by a wide margin. Combined with hyperscaler capex approaching $750B in aggregate for 2026 (per Edward Jones/FactSet) and record $489B in AI-related corporate debt issuance already this year (Goldman Sachs), the market is entering a phase where investors are demanding evidence that AI infrastructure spending converts to durable returns. Watch subsequent reports from IBM, ServiceNow, and Texas Instruments for corroborating or contradicting signals (sources: Investopedia, Yahoo Finance, Edward Jones).

Escalating U.S. trade policy actions. The Trump administration signaled it will replace expiring 10% global Section 122 tariffs (set to lapse July 24) with permanent Section 301 duties, and separately proposed a 100% tariff on imported generic pharmaceuticals beginning August 2028 (rising to 200% in 2029), explicitly aimed at reshoring generic-drug production. A new 25% tariff on Brazilian imports also took effect July 22. These actions add to an already-escalating tariff docket (including a 50% tariff on ~$20B of Canadian goods announced July 20) that raises input-cost and supply-chain risk across pharmaceuticals, consumer goods, and industrials (sources: Yahoo Finance, Edward Jones, Xinhua).

Rising long-end rates on energy-driven inflation risk. The 10-year Treasury yield climbed to its highest intraday level since May 19 (~4.67%), and the 30-year yield held above 5% for the longest stretch since the financial crisis, as oil-price gains reignited inflation concerns. CME FedWatch pricing showed the probability of a Fed rate hike at the July 28-29 meeting rising to 24% (from 11% a week earlier), with a 69% probability of a hike by September. A sustained energy-price shock colliding with a still-live hike risk is a key macro cross-current for rate-sensitive sectors (Real Estate, Utilities) and duration-heavy portfolios in the weeks ahead (sources: Investopedia, Northern Miner).
